## Detailed State Notes

### California (CA)
- **IMLC Membership:** CA is NOT an IMLC member state. Out-of-state physicians must hold a full CA medical license (Medical Board of California)
- **PSYPACT:** CA is NOT a PSYPACT member. Psychologists must hold CA license
- **NLC:** CA joined NLC effective January 1, 2024 (SB 697, 2022)
- **Platform enforcement:** CA patients can only be seen by providers holding CA license; no compact recognition for physicians
- **Audio-only Medicaid:** Covered per APL 21-011 (DHCS); requires verbal consent documentation
- **Key citation:** Cal. Health & Safety Code §1374.73 (Knox-Keene Act amendment); SB 981 (2022)

### Texas (TX)
- **IMLC Membership:** TX joined IMLC in 2017
- **Prescribing restriction:** Initial telehealth encounter may not result in prescribing without established patient relationship or referral from established provider (Tex. Occ. Code §111.005 — amended 2017 to remove in-person requirement but maintaining relationship requirement)
- **Mental health:** Texas Mental Health Parity Act under Tex. Ins. Code §§1355.001–1355.008
- **PDMP:** TX PMP (PMP InterConnect) mandatory for Schedule II–IV
- **Platform enforcement:** IMLC LOQ holders recognized; DEA registration must match TX for CS prescribing

### New York (NY)
- **IMLC Membership:** NY is NOT an IMLC member state
- **Out-of-state licensing:** NY offers a "Special Purpose License" for out-of-state practitioners in specific circumstances; not equivalent to full licensure
- **PSYPACT:** NY is NOT a PSYPACT member
- **NLC:** NY is NOT an NLC member; nurses must hold NY license
- **Platform enforcement:** NY patients can only be seen by NY-licensed providers; strictest licensing jurisdiction on this list
- **E-prescribing:** NY requires I-STOP compliance (Internet System for Tracking Over-Prescribing); all Schedule II–V must be e-prescribed

### Florida (FL)
- **IMLC Membership:** FL joined IMLC in 2019; compact LOQ recognized
- **Controlled substances:** Initial opioid prescribing requires in-person evaluation (Fla. Stat. §456.44); telehealth prescribing of opioids for new patients is prohibited
- **Telehealth standard of care:** Fla. Stat. §456.47 establishes same standard of care as in-person
- **Platform enforcement:** Block controlled substance initial Rx for new FL patients unless in-person visit documented

### Illinois (IL)
- **Behavioral health parity:** SB 1864 (2021) explicitly extends mental health and substance use parity to telehealth
- **IMLC:** IL joined in 2014; compact LOQ recognized
- **Audio-only:** IL Medicaid covers audio-only with appropriate documentation

### Washington (WA)
- **RCW 48.43.735:** Landmark early parity law (2017); requires same coverage and reimbursement as in-person
- **PSYPACT:** WA is a PSYPACT member (joined 2020)
- **Prescribing:** Telemedicine prescribing rules under WAC 246-919-610

## Interstate Compact Membership Summary

### Interstate Medical Licensure Compact (IMLC) — States in Matrix
Members (can use IMLC LOQ): TX, FL, IL, PA, OH, GA, NC, MI, NJ, VA, WA, AZ, MA, TN, IN, MO, MD, CO
Non-members (must hold state license): CA, NY

### PSYPACT Member States in Matrix
Members: TX (joined 2020), IL, PA, OH, VA, WA, AZ, CO, TN, MD, GA, NC, MO, IN
Non-members: CA, NY, FL, MI, NJ, MA

### Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) Member States in Matrix
Members: TX, FL, IL, PA, OH, GA, NC, MI, NJ, VA, WA, AZ, MA, TN, IN, MO, MD, CO
Non-members: CA (joined 2024), NY
