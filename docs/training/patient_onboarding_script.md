## Purpose and Instructions for Staff

This script guides care facility staff through the standardized process of onboarding a new patient to the FallGuard fall detection system. It ensures:
- Consistent, person-centered communication with patients and their families
- Informed consent is properly obtained and documented
- The device is correctly fitted from the first interaction
- Patient questions are answered accurately and compassionately

**Formatting guide:**
- `[STAFF ACTION]` — what you should physically do
- `[SAY]` — speak these words (adapt naturally to your communication style)
- `[PATIENT RESPONSE]` — anticipated responses; branch script as indicated
- `[DOCUMENT]` — complete this documentation step before moving on

---

## Before You Begin

`[STAFF ACTION]` Gather the following before entering the patient's room:
- [ ] FallGuard wearable unit (charged, LED solid blue = ready to pair)
- [ ] The patient's alert recipient list (pre-configured or names/numbers from care plan)
- [ ] A tablet or workstation with Care Dashboard open and logged in
- [ ] The patient's quick-start card (laminated, leave in the room after setup)
- [ ] Patient consent form (TRN-CONSENT-001) — printed or on tablet

`[STAFF ACTION]` Review the patient's chart for:
- Cognitive status (adapt communication level accordingly)
- Skin integrity concerns at potential device sites
- Any documented device preferences from previous care settings
- Presence of family or healthcare proxy if patient has limited decision-making capacity

## Script

---

### Step 1 — Introduction (Minutes 0–2)

`[STAFF ACTION]` Knock, enter with a smile. Introduce yourself if the patient does not know you.

`[SAY]`
> "Good [morning/afternoon/evening], [Patient Name]. My name is [Your Name], and I'm one of your caregivers here. Today I'd like to spend about 15 minutes with you to introduce a small device called the FallGuard — it's a wristband that helps us watch over your safety, especially if you ever have a fall. It's one of the ways we look after you while you're with us."

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — Cooperative]`
> "Okay" / "Go ahead" / "Tell me more"
→ Continue to Step 2.

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — Hesitant]`
> "I'm not sure I want that" / "Is it necessary?"
`[SAY]`
> "That's completely okay to feel uncertain. Let me explain how it works, and you can ask me any questions. You always have a say in your care, and I'll make sure we go at your pace."
→ Continue to Step 2. Do NOT pressure the patient. If they decline after the full explanation, respect their decision and document refusal (see Refusal Protocol, end of script).

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — Cannot engage (confusion, distress)]`
`[STAFF ACTION]` Pause the onboarding. Notify charge nurse. Schedule for a better time or involve the healthcare proxy/family member. Document the attempt and reason for deferral.

---

### Step 2 — Explain the Device (Minutes 2–5)

`[STAFF ACTION]` Show the patient the FallGuard device. Let them hold it if they wish.

`[SAY]`
> "This is the FallGuard wearable. It's about the size of a large watch. It sits on your wrist — like this — and it uses tiny sensors inside to notice if you have a fall. If it detects a fall, it sends an alert to our team straight away, so we can come and check on you very quickly."

`[SAY]`
> "It also has a button right here — do you see it? If you ever feel unwell or need help, you can press this button and it works like a call button. One of us will come to you."

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — "Does it hurt?"]`
`[SAY]`
> "Not at all. It's made of a soft silicone band — the same material as many fitness trackers. It just sits gently on your wrist. We'll check it regularly to make sure it's comfortable."

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — "Can I shower with it?"]`
`[SAY]`
> "Yes, you can. It's waterproof, so you can wear it in the shower or during bathing without any problem."

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — "Who gets the alert?"]`
`[SAY]`
> "The alert goes to your care team here — your assigned caregivers and the nurse on duty. It doesn't automatically contact your family, but we can let them know what happens. Would you like me to note any family members you'd like us to call if you have a fall?"

`[DOCUMENT]` Note family contact preference in care plan.

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — "Does it track where I am?"]`
`[SAY]`
> "The device does have a location feature. This is only used if you have a fall outside your room — for example, if you're on a walk and fall — so our team can find you quickly. It's not used to monitor your movements around the building. If you have concerns about this, I can note that in your care plan and we can discuss it with your care coordinator."

---

### Step 3 — Obtain Consent (Minutes 5–7)

`[SAY]`
> "Before I put the device on, I need to go over a short consent form with you. It's just a way for us to make sure you understand what the device does and that you're comfortable with wearing it. It will take about 2 minutes."

`[STAFF ACTION]` Present the consent form (TRN-CONSENT-001). Read aloud the key points:
1. The device monitors for falls using motion sensors
2. Fall alerts are sent to your care team — not to external parties without your permission
3. Location data is only accessed in the event of a fall or medical emergency
4. You may ask to have the device removed at any time
5. The device is not a substitute for regular caregiver check-ins

`[SAY]`
> "Do you have any questions about any of these points before you sign?"

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — Questions]`
`[STAFF ACTION]` Answer using the approved language from the FAQ section at the end of this script. If you cannot answer a question, say: "That's a great question — let me find out the exact answer and come back to you before we proceed." Do NOT guess.

`[SAY]`
> "If you're ready, I'd like to ask you to sign here — or if you prefer, I can read it aloud and you can give your verbal consent, which I'll document."

`[STAFF ACTION]` Obtain signature or document verbal consent. If patient lacks capacity, obtain signature from designated healthcare proxy.

`[DOCUMENT]` File consent form in patient chart. Note consent obtained, method (written/verbal/proxy), and date/time.

---

### Step 4 — Fit the Device (Minutes 7–10)

`[SAY]`
> "Great — thank you. Now let me put the device on. Which wrist feels more comfortable for you — your left or right?"

`[STAFF ACTION]` Select the correct band size (Small / Medium / Large — check against the sizing guide on the quick-start card). Clean the patient's wrist with a dry cloth.

`[SAY]`
> "I'm just going to gently slide this onto your wrist now. Let me know if it feels too tight."

`[STAFF ACTION]` Fit the device — sensor side on the inner wrist (pulse point). Secure the band (one click).

`[SAY]`
> "Can you wiggle your fingers? And does it feel comfortable? It should feel snug but not tight."

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — "It's too tight"]`
`[STAFF ACTION]` Adjust to the next larger band size. If Medium feels tight, use Large. If no size fits comfortably, notify charge nurse and document the fitting challenge. Do not force an ill-fitting device.

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — Comfortable]`
`[SAY]`
> "Perfect. Now I'm going to connect it to our system — this takes about a minute."

`[STAFF ACTION]` Pair the device to the patient's profile via Care Dashboard (see TRN-CG-001, Section 1.3). Confirm the LED turns solid green.

`[SAY]`
> "You can see the little light on the side — when it's green like that, it means it's connected and working. If you ever see it turn red, just press your call button and let us know."

`[DOCUMENT]` Record device ID, patient assignment, pairing timestamp, and band size in the patient's care plan and the Fleet Management Dashboard.

---

### Step 5 — Patient Education (Minutes 10–13)

`[SAY]`
> "Let me show you the call button one more time. This button right here — you press it once if you need help. You'll feel a small vibration when we receive your call."

`[STAFF ACTION]` Guide the patient's finger to the button. Ask them to press it as a test.

`[SAY]`
> "That's it — you pressed it. Well done. We received your test call — see, the screen here shows your name. In a real situation, we'd come right away."

`[SAY]`
> "A few things to remember:
> - Wear the device at all times, including during the night and in the shower
> - If it ever feels uncomfortable, tell any member of staff and we'll adjust it
> - You can always tell us if you'd prefer not to wear it — just let any team member know

> Is there anything about the device you're still unsure about?"

`[PATIENT RESPONSE — Additional questions]`
`[STAFF ACTION]` Address using FAQ section below. If unable to answer, escalate to charge nurse.

`[SAY]`
> "Here is your quick-start card — I'll leave it here on your bedside table. It has the main things to remember and a number to call if you have questions."

`[STAFF ACTION]` Place the laminated quick-start card on the bedside table.

---

### Step 6 — Close and Document (Minutes 13–15)

`[SAY]`
> "Thank you so much for your time and patience, [Patient Name]. You're all set up. We'll check in on you in a few hours, and remember — press that button any time you need us. Is there anything else I can help you with before I go?"

`[STAFF ACTION]` Allow the patient to ask any remaining questions or make any requests.

`[DOCUMENT]` Complete the onboarding record in Care Dashboard → Patient Assignments → [Patient Name] → Onboarding:
- Onboarding date and time
- Staff member who conducted onboarding
- Consent method (written/verbal/proxy)
- Band size and device ID
- Any patient concerns noted
- Family contact preferences
- Next check-in time

## Refusal Protocol

If a patient declines to wear the FallGuard after a complete explanation:

1. Thank the patient for listening and respect their decision without argument
2. Explain that the device is optional and their care will continue
3. Document in the care plan: Date, time, patient's stated reason, staff member present
4. Notify the charge nurse and care coordinator immediately
5. Place an alert in the patient's profile: "Fall monitoring device declined — assess fall risk with alternative measures"
6. Revisit the conversation at the next care plan review — patient preferences may change

**Never document a refusal as a non-event. It is a care safety concern requiring a compensating measure.**

## Frequently Asked Questions (Staff Reference)

| Patient Question | Approved Response |
|---|---|
| "Will this shock me?" | "No — the device only detects movement. It does not emit any electrical signals to your body." |
| "Can my family see the alerts?" | "Not automatically. We can configure the system to notify a family member you choose, if you'd like." |
| "What if the battery dies?" | "We check device battery every shift. If it's running low, we swap it with a charged device quickly — usually in less than 5 minutes." |
| "Is my information private?" | "Yes. Your data is stored securely and only accessible to your direct care team. It is never shared with anyone outside your care without your consent, except in emergencies." |
| "Can I see my own data?" | "You can ask any staff member to show you your alert history on our dashboard. We're happy to share that with you." |
| "What if I fall in the bathroom?" | "The device works everywhere in your room, including the bathroom and shower. It's waterproof and stays connected." |
